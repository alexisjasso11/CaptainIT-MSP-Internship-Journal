## What I Did

### Morning — Ticket Review & Troubleshooting Analysis
- Pulled up tickets assigned to other technicians in ConnectWise
- Read through a variety of real client issues across different categories
- For each ticket, thought through how I would approach diagnosing and resolving the issue
- Built familiarity with how tickets are written, categorized, and escalated in an MSP environment

### Afternoon — On-Site Field Visit (Client Location)
> **Note:** This visit was self-initiated — I noticed a technician preparing to leave for a client site and proactively asked if I could tag along. Taking that initiative on Day 2 led to my first real on-site MSP experience.

- Traveled to a client's site alongside a senior technician
- Conducted a **network discovery** — identifying and cataloging devices connected to the client's network
- **Tagged employee laptops** with asset labels for inventory and tracking purposes
- **Mapped out security cameras and access points (APs)** across the site for reference documentation
- Observed how an MSP technician interacts with a client on-site professionally

---

## What I Learned
Reviewing tickets gave me a real look at the variety of issues an MSP handles daily — from simple user errors to more complex network and software problems. The field visit reinforced that a big part of IT support is documentation and asset management, not just fixing things. Mapping cameras and APs was a reminder that knowing the physical layout of a network is just as important as understanding it logically.

Most importantly — asking to tag along instead of waiting to be invited made all the difference. Proactivity is a skill in itself.
