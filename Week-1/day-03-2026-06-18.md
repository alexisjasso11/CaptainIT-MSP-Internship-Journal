### On-Site Visit #1 — Dock & Dual Monitor Issue
> First on-site visit of the day. Client's ThinkPad dock was not outputting video to either of the two connected monitors.

- Client had previously been advised that a **Thunderbolt 3/4 USB-C cable** was required to deliver both power and video data from the dock to the laptop
- Client purchased a replacement cable, but the issue persisted — no display output
- On-site troubleshooting steps:
  - Confirmed the dock was receiving power
  - Updated dock firmware (did not resolve the issue)
  - Ran **Dell Command Update** to check for driver/firmware updates (did not resolve the issue)
  - **Identified the root cause:** the client had purchased a standard USB-C charging cable, not a Thunderbolt 3/4 cable capable of carrying video data
- Implemented a temporary fix: connected both monitors via **VGA-to-HDMI** and **HDMI-to-USB-C** adapters so the client could continue working while the correct cable is ordered

### On-Site Visit #2 — PC Not Populating Remotely / Black Monitor
> Second on-site visit of the day. Client reported their PC was not showing up for remote access, and that the monitor appeared black even though the PC was powered on.

- Arrived on-site and found the monitor had come back on
- Troubleshooting steps performed:
  - Checked all cable connections for looseness
  - Reviewed **Event Viewer** logs for related errors
  - Verified power and sleep settings on the device
- No further issues found — remote access connectivity was restored and confirmed working

## What I Learned
Today reinforced that troubleshooting isn't always about running diagnostics — sometimes it's about double-checking the basics that get overlooked, like confirming the *exact* cable spec a client actually purchased. Firmware and software updates won't fix a hardware compatibility issue. I also learned the practical difference between a standard USB-C cable and a Thunderbolt 3/4 USB-C cable, and how to work around a hardware limitation with adapters as a temporary fix. Continuing to ask for tickets and tag along on visits is paying off — today was noticeably more hands-on than Days 1 and 2.
