## What I Did

### Morning — Ticket Review
- Continued reviewing tickets assigned to other technicians to study troubleshooting approaches

### Ticket #1 — Email & Shared Drive Access (First Assigned Ticket)
> Client requested email access be granted to a coworker covering for an employee out on leave. The ticket was later updated to also request access to a shared network drive.

- Granted the covering employee access to the absent employee's email
- Attempted to map the shared network drive — first time doing this task, so did some independent research first
- A coworker stepped in to walk me through the correct process:
  - Opened the **directory services** admin panel and added the user under the appropriate permissions group
  - Remoted into the client's desktop via **remote desktop**
  - Mapped the shared network drive through **File Explorer**
- Ticket wasn't technically difficult, but it introduced a new process — plan to practice mapping network drives independently in my home lab to reinforce it

### Ticket #2 — Cloud License Correction
- Client requested license corrections across their organization
- Logged into the **cloud admin portal**
- Reassigned correct licenses to specific users and removed licenses from users who no longer needed them

## What I Learned
Getting my first two tickets felt like a real shift from observing to doing. Mapping a network drive through directory services and remote desktop was new, and having a coworker walk me through it once means I now have a process I can repeat and practice on my own. I also learned that simple-sounding tickets, like a license correction, still require careful attention — accidentally licensing or unlicensing the wrong user could create a real problem for the client. Ending Week 1 having owned two tickets start to finish is a solid marker of progress from Day 1.
