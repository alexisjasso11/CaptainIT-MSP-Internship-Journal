## What I Did

### Location 1 — Client Office: SonicWall TZ80 Deployment

> Deployed the SonicWall TZ80 firewall at the client's office to replace the existing gateway/router.

- Swapped out the previous gateway/router and installed the SonicWall TZ80 firewall
- Immediately encountered an issue — all office devices were failing to reconnect to the network
- **Root cause:** devices were holding onto stale DHCP leases and DNS entries from the previous router and not automatically requesting new ones from the SonicWall's DHCP server
- **Resolution:** ran the following on each affected machine:
  - `ipconfig /release` — released the old DHCP lease
  - `ipconfig /renew` — requested a new IP address from the SonicWall
  - `ipconfig /flushdns` — cleared stale DNS cache entries
- Applied the fix across all 5–6 office computers — all devices successfully reconnected

### Location 2 — Boss's Home: IPsec Tunnel Completion

> Traveled to the boss's home to install the second SonicWall TZ80 and complete the site-to-site IPsec VPN tunnel.

- Swapped the existing home gateway/router with the second SonicWall TZ80 unit
- Ran `ipconfig /release`, `/renew`, and `flushdns` to reconnect home devices
- The IPsec tunnel between the office SonicWall and the home SonicWall came up immediately and without issue
- Confirmed the boss could remotely access his office PC through the secure tunnel
- Setup went flawlessly — a direct result of the firewall having been properly pre-configured the day before

## What I Learned
Today proved that pre-configuration and lab preparation matters in production. The SonicWall TZ80 I configured yesterday worked exactly as expected in both locations — the hard part wasn't the firewall, it was the client environment around it. Stale DHCP leases are a common and easily overlooked issue during any router/gateway swap, and knowing to run ipconfig /release /renew /flushdns immediately is now something I'll check first in any network cutover. The home deployment being seamless was a confidence builder — I configured that firewall and IPsec tunnel myself, and it worked in a live environment without needing to be reworked.
