  - Escalated to a senior technician back at the office and received approval to **temporarily disable the GEO IP filter**

## What I Learned
GEO IP filtering is a real security control that blocks authentication attempts from IP addresses outside of allowed regions — and it can catch legitimate traffic just as easily as malicious traffic if not configured correctly. My allow rule attempt was the right instinct, but the issue may have needed a more specific rule or a different approach entirely — something worth researching further. I also learned the importance of proper escalation: rather than guessing or making changes I wasn't authorized to make, I contacted a senior tech and got proper approval before toggling a security setting. On the offboarding side, I learned that wiping a device isn't just hitting a button — data extraction and documentation (SOPing) come first to make sure nothing important is lost and everything is accounted for.
