## What I Did

### On-Site Desktop Replacement & Upgrade
> Traveled with a field technician to a client site to replace a desktop that needed upgrading.

- Swapped out the old desktop for a new upgraded machine at the client's location
- Replicated the user's exact setup on the new device — applications, preferences, and configuration
- Majority of the migration went smoothly with only minor bumps along the way

### Lumm Software — License Issue & Vendor Escalation
> Lumm is a specialized application used for union documentation. Turned out to be the most time-consuming part of the day.

- Attempted to install and activate **Lumm** on the new desktop
- The application required a license that did not transfer automatically to the new machine
- Troubleshot the licensing issue but were unable to resolve it independently
- **Escalated to Lumm's support team** — a Lumm support technician remoted into the machine and resolved the licensing activation directly
- Lumm was confirmed fully working after the vendor's remote session

---

## What I Learned
Not every piece of software an MSP supports is something they can fully troubleshoot on their own — specialized or niche applications often have vendor-specific licensing systems that require direct support escalation. Knowing when to stop troubleshooting and hand off to a vendor is a skill, not a failure. I also picked up that replicating a user's exact setup during a hardware swap is more involved than it looks — small details like application configurations, shortcuts, and preferences all matter to the end user even if they seem minor from the tech's side.
