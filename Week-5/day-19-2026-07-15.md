## What I Did

### On-Site Printer Support — Two Printers, Two Problems

**Printer 1 — Network Troubleshooting (Senior Tech)**
- Senior technician diagnosed Printer 1 as a network connectivity issue
- Worked through network-level troubleshooting to identify and resolve the problem
- Observed the process and learned how printer network issues are diagnosed at the infrastructure level

**Printer 2 — Disconnecting & Slow (Self-Resolved)**
- Printer 2 was intermittently disconnecting and running very slowly
- Senior tech was occupied with Printer 1 — took initiative to troubleshoot Printer 2 independently
- Looked up HP's official installation documentation for the printer model
- Downloaded the **HP Smart application**, which automatically detected the printer on the network
- HP Smart resolved the connectivity and performance issues quickly — printer confirmed fully working
- First time independently resolving a printer issue on-site without guidance

---

## What I Learned
Now I understand firsthand why IT teams dread printers — they're time-consuming, inconsistent, and rarely straightforward. Two printers at the same client site had two completely different root causes requiring two completely different approaches. The biggest takeaway from Printer 2 is that sometimes the fastest fix isn't deep manual troubleshooting — it's going straight to the manufacturer's own tools, which are built specifically to detect and resolve their hardware. Knowing when to use a vendor utility versus digging into settings manually is a judgement call that comes with experience, and today was a good first step toward building that instinct.
